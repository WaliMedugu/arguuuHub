Section 03

Data We Collect

We collect the minimum data necessary to provide a functional and safe experience. Below is a complete breakdown of all data categories we collect.

3.1 — Account & Identity Data

  • Email address (used for account creation, authentication, and service communications)
  • Username (publicly visible, chosen by you at registration)
  • Profile avatar or display initials (generated from username if no avatar is uploaded)

  • Right to Rectification — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data

Section 08

Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • All data in transit is encrypted via TLS 1.2 or higher
  • All data at rest is encrypted by Supabase using AES-256
  • Row Level Security (RLS) policies on all Supabase database tables to ensure users can only access data they are authorised to see
  • Authentication tokens are short-lived and rotated regularly
  • We never store service role or administrative API keys in client-side code
  • Periodic security reviews of our codebase and infrastructure configurations

No system is completely impenetrable. In the event of a data breach, we will notify affected users and, where required, the NDPC within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

Getting Started

Arguuu is a structured real-time debate application built for people who love making arguments, dissecting ideas, and competing intellectually. Two users debate a topic — one FOR the motion, one AGAINST — while an audience watches, reacts, and votes on who argued best. Performance is tracked via the WIT (Win-Intelligence-Track) score, which builds over time across all your debates.

Anyone aged 13 and above can use Arguuu. Users between 13–17 must have parental or guardian consent. The platform is designed for thoughtful, civil discourse — no prior debate experience required.

Yes, the core Arguuu experience is completely free. All debate features, matchmaking, WIT scoring, and audience participation are available at no cost. If we introduce premium features in the future, we will notify users clearly and in advance.

Arguuu is available as a native mobile application for Android and iOS devices. It is built with Flutter, which means the experience is consistent and smooth across both platforms.

How Debates Work

You can browse or create Debate Rooms from the home screen. Once inside a room, you can join as a debater (FOR or AGAINST) or as an audience member. Matchmaking will automatically pair you with an opponent when one is available. You'll receive a notification when a match is found and will be taken to a countdown screen before the debate begins.

Each message (called a "chat bubble") has a maximum length of 200 characters and must contain at least 1 word. You may send a maximum of 3 bubbles per turn. These constraints are intentional — they reward concise, punchy argumentation over rambling. Turns alternate between debaters, so strategy is key.

Tagging lets you highlight specific text within your opponent's message to draw attention to it — useful for pointing out logical fallacies, contradictions, or key admissions. Audience members can see tagged segments and they contribute to the overall analysis of the debate.

Yes. You may resign from a debate at any time. A resignation is recorded as a loss and will affect your WIT score accordingly. Frequent or unexplained resignations may also affect your matchmaking priority. Always try to complete debates — sportsmanship is a core value of the Arguuu community.

At the end of a debate, audience members enter a voting screen and select either: the FOR debater, the AGAINST debater, or "Add 5 Minutes" (to extend the debate). Each vote carries a weight multiplier (e.g., 1.2×) based on the voter's credibility score. The weighted tally determines the winner. Debaters cannot vote on their own debates.

WIT Score

WIT stands for Win-Intelligence-Track. It is Arguuu's proprietary ranking metric that evaluates your debate performance over time. Factors influencing your WIT score include: debate wins and losses (weighted by opponent strength), audience vote margins, completion rate (non-resignations), and participation frequency. Your WIT score grows with consistent, quality debating.

Your WIT score decreases naturally from losses and resignations — that's by design. However, scores will only be forcibly reset by us in cases of confirmed cheating, vote manipulation, or account reinstatement following a ban. We will notify you if this occurs. Deleting your account permanently deletes your WIT score.
